Ingredients

    For Frying the Cauliflower:


  • 1 medium cauliflower (cut into florets)
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ¼ cup cornflour
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder
  • ½ cup water (or as needed)
  • Oil (for deep frying)


    • For the Manchurian Sauce:


  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (finely chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 small onion (cubed)
  • 1 small capsicum (cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon red chili sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cornflour mixed with ¼ cup water (for thickening, only for gravy version)
  • 1 tablespoon spring onions (chopped, for garnish)


Instructions


  1. Bring water to a boil, add the cauliflower florets, and blanch for 2 minutes. Drain and pat dry.
    2. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, black pepper, salt, red chili powder, and water to make a smooth batter. Dip the cauliflower florets in the batter and coat evenly. Heat oil in a pan and deep-fry the florets until golden brown and crispy. Drain and set aside.

    Step 2: Prepare the Manchurian Sauce


  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add chopped ginger, garlic, and green chili. Sauté for a few seconds.
  3. Add cubed onions and capsicum, stir-fry on high heat for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Pour in soy sauce, red chili sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, black pepper, salt, and sugar (if using). Mix well.
  5. For the gravy version: Add the cornflour-water mixture and let it thicken for 1-2 minutes.


    6. Step 3: Assemble the Dish


  6. Add the crispy cauliflower to the sauce and toss well to coat.
  7. Garnish with chopped spring onions and serve hot.
