Ingredients

    For the Puris (Crispy Shells):


  • 1 cup (125g) semolina (sooji)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

    For the Spicy Pani (Flavored Water):


  • 1 cup (25g) fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup (10g) fresh coriander leaves
  • 1 small green chili (adjust to taste)
  • 1-inch piece ginger
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon black salt
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 3 cups chilled water
  • Salt (to taste)

    • For the Filling:


  • 2 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • Salt (to taste)

Instructions

    Step 1: Prepare the Puris


  1. In a bowl, mix semolina, flour, and salt. Add water gradually to make a stiff dough. Cover and let it rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Roll out the dough thinly and cut small circles using a round cutter or bottle cap.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the puris on medium heat until they puff up and turn golden brown. Drain on paper towels.

    4. Step 2: Prepare the Pani


  4. Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chili, ginger, cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, tamarind pulp, and lemon juice with a little water.
  5. Strain the mixture and add 3 cups of chilled water. Adjust seasoning if needed. Refrigerate for 30 minutes for better flavor.

    6. Step 3: Prepare the Filling


  6. Mix mashed potatoes, chickpeas, chaat masala, cumin powder, and salt.

    7. Step 4: Assemble & Serve


  7. Crack open a puri, add some filling, and pour the spicy pani into it.
  8. Enjoy immediately before the puris turn soggy!
