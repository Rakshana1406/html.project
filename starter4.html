Ingredients

    For the Batter:


  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda (optional, for fluffiness)
  • ½ cup water (or as needed to make a thick batter)


    • For the Paneer:


  • 200g paneer (cut into cubes or slices)
  • 1 teaspoon chat masala (for seasoning)


    • For Frying:


  • Oil (for deep frying)


Instructions

    Step 1: Prepare the Batter


  1. In a mixing bowl, combine besan, rice flour, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, ajwain, salt, and baking soda.
  2. Gradually add water and whisk to make a thick, smooth batter. Ensure no lumps remain.

    3. Step 2: Coat the Paneer


  3. Sprinkle chat masala over the paneer cubes for extra flavor
  4. Dip each paneer cube into the batter, coating it evenly.

    5. Step 3: Fry the Pakoras


  5. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  6. Carefully drop the batter-coated paneer cubes into the hot oil.
  7. Fry until golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally.
  8. Remove and drain on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.

    9. Step 4: Serve


  9. Serve hot with mint chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup.
