Ingredients

    For the Dough:


  • 2 cups (250g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) water (as needed)

    For the Filling:


  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled, peeled, and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup (75g) green peas (boiled)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for cooking filling)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

    Step 1: Prepare the Dough


  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and oil/ghee.
  2. Rub the oil into the flour with your fingers until it forms a crumbly texture.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.

    4. Step 2: Prepare the Filling


  4. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  5. Add ginger, green chili, and peas. Saut√© for a minute.
  6. Add mashed potatoes, garam masala, coriander powder, turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add lemon juice and chopped cilantro. Let it cool completely.

    8. Step 3:Shape & Fry the Samosas


  8. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each into a small circle (about 6 inches in diameter).
  9. Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles.
  10. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edge with water.
  11. Fill the cone with potato filling, then seal the top by pressing the edges together.
  12. Heat oil in a deep pan on medium-low heat. Fry samosas in batches until golden brown and crispy.
  13. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with chutney.
