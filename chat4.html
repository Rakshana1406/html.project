Ingredients

    For the Puri (Crispy Shells):


  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

    • For the Masala Gravy:


  • 1 cup dried white peas (soaked overnight & boiled)
  • 2 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black salt
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro
  • 4 cups water (adjust consistency)

    • For Garnishing:


  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • Sev (optional, for crunch)

Instructions

    Step 1: Prepare the Puris


  1. Mix semolina, flour, and salt in a bowl. Add water gradually to knead a stiff dough. Let it rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Roll out small circles and deep-fry in hot oil until crispy. Drain on paper towels.

    3. Step 2: Prepare the Masala Gravy


  3. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pan, add onions, and saut√© until golden.
  4. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  5. Add tomato puree, turmeric, chili powder, cumin, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates.
  6. Mash the boiled white peas and potatoes, then add them to the pan with 4 cups of water.
  7. Stir in tamarind pulp, garam masala, and chaat masala. Simmer for 10 minutes.

    8. Step 3: Assemble & Serve


  8. Crush puris in a serving plate and pour hot masala gravy over them.
  9. Top with chopped onions, tomatoes, cilantro, chaat masala, and sev.
  10. Serve immediately and enjoy!
